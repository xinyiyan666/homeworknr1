Every ginger cat is unique, but because they all carry the tabby gene, they have certain characteristics in common. For example, all tabbies have a distinctive M-shaped marking on their foreheads. What is a ginger cat?

A cat who's fur lands anywhere along the color spectrum from pale yellow to orange to deep red. While gingers don’t carry the questionably sinister reputation of black cats, or the haughty, aloof personality of Persians, their orange purr-sonas can be a complicated mix of several feline traits. It’s in their genes. Whether you call your ginger purrball a cinnamon, red, marmalade or tiger cat.
